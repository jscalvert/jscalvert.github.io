I’m a mathematician and data scientist. I’m interested in the relationship between the behavior of individuals and that of the collectives they comprise. Fundamental scientific questions concern the “forward” direction (E.g., “How do the properties of tissues emerge from the properites of cells?”). Grand challenges of engineering concern the reverse (E.g., “How do I design an individual robot so that the swarm has desirable collective behavior?”). I develop theory and methods to answer questions like these.

Currently, I’m a postdoctoral fellow at Georgia Tech and a visiting postdoctoral fellow at the Santa Fe Institute. I spent Spring 2025 as the Berlekamp postdoctoral fellow at the Simons Laufer Mathematical Sciences Institute in Berkeley, CA. For details on my background and experience as a professional data scientist, see About. For more on my research, check out my Google Scholar profile, Papers, or Posts.
